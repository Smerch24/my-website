History of Formation

In 1962, the construction of a nitrogen fertilizer plant began in the city of Cherkasy, which later received the name -
Cherkasy
chemical plant (Today - PrJSC "Azot").

In accordance with the Decree of the Council of Ministers of the USSR of November 7, 1963 No. 1210, part 1, the
executive committee of Cherkasy
of the City Council allocated a plot of land with an area of ​​2.04 hectares, on which the construction of a technical
school began in 1964,
which was supposed to provide workers for the future industrial giant of Ukraine.

The educational institution was opened in 1966 in accordance with the order of the State Committee of the Council of
Ministers of the Ukrainian SSR on
of vocational and technical education No. 57 of May 4, 1966 at the production base of the Cherkasy Chemical Plant. The
first name is
technical school No. 2 of Cherkasy (Order of the Cherkasy Regional Department of Vocational and Technical Education of
May 10, 1966
year #47). In the future, the institution was reorganized: in 1984, it became Secondary Vocational and Technical School
No. 10 of Cherkasy
(order of the Cherkasy Regional Department of Vocational and Technical Education dated August 10, 1984 No. 212), in 1991
- above
vocational school No. 10 in Cherkasy (Order of the Ministry of National Education of the Ukrainian SSR dated April 4,
1991 No. 82).

Educational building No. 2 was completed in 1985, in 2021 the reconstruction of educational building No. 1 was
completed.

Barabash Vsevolod Omelyanovych (1966-1967), deputy head of the department, was appointed the first director of the
school
